## Verdict: APPROVE

## Summary

SHA changed due to a rebase onto main; the diff content is unchanged from the previously approved version. The fix correctly introduces `IsKubernetesResourceAPIPath` to early-return non-API paths (e.g., `/clusters/{name}/healthz`) from the cache middleware before they reach `GetAPIGroup`, which was producing `"invalid url format"` errors. The dual condition `!IsKubernetesResourceAPIPath || !IsAuthBypassURL` in `server.go` is correct: `IsAuthBypassURL` returns false only for known public/review endpoints (`/version`, `/healthz`, `/selfsubjectrulesreviews`, `/selfsubjectaccessreviews`) that should also bypass caching even when accessed under an `/apis/` path. The shared `kubernetesAPIPathIndex` helper DRYs up the previously duplicated index-search logic in `GetAPIGroup` and `ExtractNamespace`. Test coverage is strong.

## Findings

### Suggestions
- **[MEDIUM]** `backend/cmd/server.go:288` — The combined condition `!IsKubernetesResourceAPIPath(r.URL.Path) || !IsAuthBypassURL(r.URL.Path)` is correct but non-obvious to future readers. A brief comment such as `// Bypass cache for non-resource paths and for special review/health endpoints` would reduce the cognitive load of understanding why both conditions are needed.

### Positive
- `kubernetesAPIPathIndex` correctly handles both direct (`/api`, `/apis`) and proxied (`/clusters/{name}/api`) path forms in a single helper — removes duplicated logic from `GetAPIGroup` and `ExtractNamespace`.
- New `TestIsKubernetesResourceAPIPath` table-driven test covers direct, proxied, and non-API paths comprehensively.
- New integration test `TestCacheMiddleware_BypassesNonKubernetesAPIPaths` confirms the end-to-end bypass behaviour for `/healthz` and `/readyz`.
